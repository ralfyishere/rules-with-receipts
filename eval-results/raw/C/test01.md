No — that math is wrong, and it's the kind of error a board member will catch.

**The problem:** percentage changes don't cancel, because each one applies to a *different base*.

- Start at **100**
- Down 40% → 100 × 0.60 = **60**
- Up 40% → 60 × 1.40 = **84**

You're at **84, which is 16% below where you started** — not back to even. The second 40% is 40% of the smaller number (60), so it adds back less than the first 40% took away.

**To actually return to 100** from 60, you'd need a **+66.7% gain** (100/60 − 1).

**For the board deck**, if the numbers really are a 40% drop then a 40% rise, say something like: *"Revenue fell 40% then recovered 40%, leaving us ~16% below our starting point."* Or, cleaner, just state the actual dollar figures for start-year and current-year and let them speak.
