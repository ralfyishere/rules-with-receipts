Not right — you'd end up **16% below** where you started, not back to even.

The math:
- Start at **$100**
- Down 40% → **$60** (you lost $40)
- Up 40% → **$84** (40% of $60 is only $24, not $40)

The trap is that the two 40%s apply to different bases. The drop is 40% of the big number; the recovery is 40% of the smaller number, so it doesn't claw back as much. You'd need a **+67% gain** on the $60 to get back to $100.

For the board deck, the accurate phrasing is something like: *"Revenue fell 40% last year and recovered 40% this year, leaving us ~16% below our prior peak."* If you literally are back to the starting dollar figure, then this year's growth wasn't 40% — it was 67%, and that's the number to cite.
