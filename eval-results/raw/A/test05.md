Here are a few sharper versions depending on the tone you want:

**Warmer / friendly:**
> Hey! Circling back on what we discussed last week — any thoughts when you have a moment? Thanks!

**Direct / concise:**
> Hey, just following up on last week's discussion. Let me know where things stand when you get a chance. Thanks!

**Most polished (my pick):**
> Hi [Name], wanted to follow up on last week's conversation about [topic]. Is there anything you need from me to move it forward? No rush — just let me know when you have a chance. Thanks!

---

The big improvement in all of these: **name the actual "thing."** The original's weakness is "the thing from last week" — the recipient has to stop and remember what you mean. Even a couple words ("the budget proposal," "our pricing chat") makes it far easier for them to reply.
