No — it's off, and it's worth catching before it hits the board.

Percentage changes don't cancel out, because the second percentage applies to a smaller base.

Walk it through with $100:
- Down 40% → $100 × 0.60 = **$60**
- Up 40% → $60 × 1.40 = **$84**

So you'd be at **84% of where you started — down 16%**, not back to even.

The reason: the 40% drop is off the original ($40), but the 40% gain is off the reduced $60 (only $24). Smaller base, smaller dollar gain.

To actually return to the starting point after a 40% drop, you'd need to grow **~66.7%** (1 ÷ 0.60 = 1.667).

Suggested rewrite for the deck:
> "Revenue fell 40% last year and recovered 40% this year, leaving us ~16% below our prior peak."

Or if the real story is that dollar revenue *is* flat and the "40%" figures are loose, check the actual numbers — those two framings tell very different stories to a board, so it's worth pinning down which one is true.
