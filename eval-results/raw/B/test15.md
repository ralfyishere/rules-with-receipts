The two 20%s don't cancel because they're taken on different bases:

- Start: **$100** (original)
- After 20% discount: 100 × 0.80 = **$80**
- After 20% service fee: 80 × 1.20 = **$96**

You end at **96% of the original — a 4% net reduction**, not cost-neutral.

The reason is that the 20% fee is applied to the *already-discounted* $80, so it only adds back $16, while the discount removed $20. A discount and a surcharge of the same percentage never cancel; the result is always `1 − r²` of the original (here 1 − 0.2² = 0.96), i.e. slightly below the starting price regardless of the rate.

If you actually want it to be cost-neutral, the service fee would need to be **25%** (80 × 1.25 = 100).
