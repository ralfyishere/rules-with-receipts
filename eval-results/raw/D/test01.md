**It's wrong — you'd be down ~16%, not back to even.** Don't put that line in the deck.

The reason: the two percentages apply to *different bases*, so they don't cancel.

- Start at 100.
- Drop 40% → you lose 40, leaving **60**.
- Grow 40% → but now 40% is of 60, not 100. That's +24, giving **84**.

So you'd end at **84 — about 16% below where you started.** The second 40% is a smaller number because it's growing off a shrunken base.

**Rule of thumb:** an X% drop needs *more* than an X% gain to recover. To get from 60 back to 100 you'd need +67%, not +40%.

If the actual figures are a real 40% decline followed by a real 40% recovery, the honest board line is something like: *"Revenue fell 40% then recovered 40%, leaving us ~16% below our starting point"* — or state the actual dollar figures start and end, which sidesteps the percentage trap entirely.

One caveat worth checking before you finalize: confirm whether "grew 40% this year" was measured off *last year's depressed number* (the case above → you're at 84) or was quoted as "back to the original level" by someone who already did the recovery math (in which case the underlying figure, not the 40%, is what belongs in the deck). The two 40%s canceling is the classic trap — verify which base each was calculated from.
