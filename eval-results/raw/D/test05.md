> **Hi [Name] — checking in on [the thing] we discussed last week. Any thoughts on next steps, or a rough timeline for when you'll have a chance to look? Happy to help move it along. Thanks!**

What changed and why:

- **Named the topic.** "The thing from last week" makes the reader dig through memory or old threads. Filling in the actual subject (a proposal, an intro, a decision) is the single biggest improvement — it's why follow-ups get ignored.
- **Gave a concrete ask.** "Let me know when you get a chance" has no action in it. "Any thoughts on next steps, or a timeline?" tells them exactly what a useful reply looks like.
- **Offered to help.** Shifts the tone from chasing to collaborating, which makes people more likely to respond.

Two quick calibrations:

- **If it's genuinely low-stakes and casual**, your original is fine — just swap in the topic: *"Hey, following up on the [budget draft] from last week — let me know when you get a chance. Thanks!"*
- **If it's time-sensitive**, add a soft deadline: *"...hoping to lock this in by Thursday — does that work on your end?"*
