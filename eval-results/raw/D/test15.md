**The math:** take an original price of $100.
- 20% discount → $80
- Add a 20% service fee on that $80 → $80 × 1.20 = **$96**

You end at **$96, not $100**. The promotion is a net **4% discount**, not cost-neutral.

**Why it doesn't cancel:** the two 20%s are taken on *different bases*. The discount comes off the full price ($100), but the fee is charged on the already-reduced price ($80). A smaller base means the fee adds fewer dollars than the discount removed. The gap is always the product of the two rates: 20% × 20% = 4% of the original.

**General rule:** a −x% change followed by a +x% change always nets to a loss of x²/100 percent. It's never cost-neutral for any nonzero rate.

To actually get back to the original price after a 20% discount, you'd need a fee of **25%** (100/80 − 1), not 20%.
