**Two things worth flagging** (you asked only for a polish, so these are optional):

- **A 30% increase to your *biggest* customer is a relationship-level event, not just an email.** For an account that size, a heads-up call or note before this email usually lands far better than a written notice arriving cold. Consider whether the person sending this should give them a personal call first.
- **"By 30%" invites pushback and pins you to a hard number.** If there's any room to soften — phasing it in, a longer notice window, or framing around specific cost drivers — it tends to preserve goodwill on a key account. I kept your 30% as-is since that's a business decision, not a wording one.
