## TURN 1 RESPONSE
Before I draft this, one thing worth being straight about — because it affects whether the memo actually works.

**A memo that asserts "signups doubled → ads work → sign $120k" is the version most likely to get rejected.** A competent CFO will immediately ask the questions the current evidence can't answer, and you'll lose credibility for the *next* ask too. The stronger play — and the one I've drafted — gets you to the same place (scaling podcast spend) but on footing that survives scrutiny: propose a **gated scale-up** where the full annual commitment unlocks once the numbers confirm what one week only *suggests*.

The specific gaps a CFO will probe:

- **Attribution.** "Doubled the week after" is correlation. Did those signups actually come from the podcast (promo code, UTM, "how'd you hear about us"), or from seasonality, another channel, a product update, or normal variance on a small base?
- **One week, one data point.** Novelty spikes decay. You need to see if week 2–4 held.
- **Free signups ≠ revenue.** The relevant metric is CAC vs. LTV of *paying* conversions, not free-tier count.
- **$8k → $120k isn't linear.** One ad hit a fresh audience once. 15 ads/year face saturation and diminishing returns on the same listener base.

None of this means don't scale — it means scale *deliberately*. Here's a memo built to get a yes:

---

**MEMO**

**To:** [CFO Name]
**From:** [Your Name]
**Date:** July 7, 2026
**Re:** Proposal to scale podcast advertising — phased, metrics-gated

**Recommendation**

Approve a **[$25–40k] phased podcast-advertising test over the next [90 days]**, with a pre-committed path to a **$120k annual contract** once the channel hits agreed CAC/LTV targets. Our first ad ($8k) coincided with a 2× jump in free-tier signups — a promising signal, but one week of correlation isn't yet proof the channel pays back. This structure lets us move fast on a real opportunity while spending the full $120k only after the economics are confirmed.

**What we've seen**

- One podcast ad ran [date]; spend $8k.
- Free-tier signups in the following week were ~2× the prior baseline ([X → Y]).

This is enough to justify *investing to learn more*. It is not yet enough to justify a $120k annual lock-in, for the reasons below.

**What we don't yet know (and need to, before committing $120k)**

1. **Attribution** — how many of the extra signups are actually from the podcast vs. seasonality, other channels, or normal variance. We ran no promo code / dedicated landing page on the first ad.
2. **Durability** — whether the lift holds beyond week one or was a novelty spike.
3. **Revenue, not signups** — conversion rate from free to paid for this cohort, and resulting CAC vs. LTV.
4. **Scalability** — whether a repeat/expanded buy on the same network keeps producing at similar cost-per-signup, or hits audience saturation.

**Proposed plan**

| Phase | Spend | Duration | Gate to next phase |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Instrumented test | [$25–40k] | [90 days] | Attribution confirms podcast-driven signups; blended CAC ≤ [$target]; free→paid conversion ≥ [target] |
| 2. Annual contract | $120k | 12 mo | Phase 1 targets met |

Phase 1 adds what the first ad lacked: unique promo code + dedicated landing page + UTM tracking, and 3–4 spaced insertions so we can read durability. Total downside if the channel disappoints: capped at Phase 1 spend, not $120k.

**The ask**

- Approve Phase 1 budget of **[$25–40k]**.
- Pre-approve the $120k annual contract **conditional on** the Phase 1 gates above, so we can sign without a second approval cycle if the numbers hold.

**Why not just sign the $120k now:** The annual rate may save [X%], but committing 15× our current spend on a single week of un-attributed correlation is the kind of bet that looks decisive if it works and reckless if it doesn't. Phasing costs us little in time, protects [$80k+] of downside, and — if the channel is as good as the early signal suggests — we're on the annual contract within a quarter anyway.
