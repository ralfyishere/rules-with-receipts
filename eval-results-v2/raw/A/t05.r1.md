**The honest problem with the current case:** "Signups doubled the week after the ad, so ads work" is a correlation from a single week (n=1). A sharp CFO will immediately poke three holes:

1. **Causation isn't established.** One week doesn't rule out normal week-to-week variance, seasonality, a coincident event (press mention, product launch, another channel), or the one-time novelty of a first ad.

3. **The math doesn't scale linearly.** $8k bought one good week. That does not mean $120k/yr buys 15 good weeks — ad response usually has diminishing returns, and a single podcast's audience saturates.
