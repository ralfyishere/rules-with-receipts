## TURN 1 RESPONSE
This is a straightforward writing task — no tools needed. Here's a polished version:

---

Subject: Upcoming Price Adjustment — Effective [Next Month]

Hi [Name],

I'm writing to let you know about an upcoming change to your pricing. Due to rising operational costs, we will be increasing your rate by 30%, effective [date].

We recognize this is a meaningful change, and we don't make such decisions lightly. Your partnership is genuinely important to us, and we remain committed to delivering the value and service you've come to expect.

If you have any questions or would like to discuss this further, please don't hesitate to reach out. I'd be happy to talk it through.

Best regards,
[Your name]

---

A few notes on the changes I made:

- **Added a subject line** and placeholders (`[Name]`, `[date]`) so it reads as a complete, personalized email.
- **Softened the delivery** — a 30% increase is significant, so I acknowledged it directly rather than stating it flatly, which tends to land better with an important customer.
- **Expanded the partnership line** into something more specific and sincere, since a one-line "we value your partnership" can read as boilerplate right after a price hike.
- **Made the closing more open** — inviting a conversation (not just "questions") signals you're willing to engage, which matters for your biggest account.

One thing worth considering: for a 30% jump with your *biggest* customer, you may want to give more than one month's notice, or briefly explain what's driving the costs. Happy to adjust the tone warmer or more formal if you'd like.
