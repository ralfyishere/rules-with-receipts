One thing worth flagging: **a 30% increase to your biggest customer is a significant jump**, and the polished wording doesn't change that reality. A few things you may want to consider before sending:

- **Give more lead time.** "Effective next month" is short notice for a major account. 60–90 days is more standard for an increase of this size and reads as more respectful.
- **Justify beyond "rising costs."** For your largest customer, it's worth being specific or pairing the increase with value they're getting — otherwise "rising costs" can feel like your problem being passed to them.
- **Consider phasing or a heads-up call.** A 30% jump landing by email may catch them off guard. A short call before the written notice often preserves the relationship better.
